Questions and Answers

What is this?
This is a way to improve your immersion in PTFS by calculating your aircraft's performance capabilities before takeoff and landing. If you have a long runway, you usually do not require the entire runway to take off. Instead, you can use a lower thrust setting. Alternatively, you can depart from a holding point that is closer to your gate.

What do the takeoff numbers mean?
For takeoff, the calculation gives you four values. The thrust setting is the minimum amount of thrust required to depart safely. V1 is the speed at which there is no longer enough runway left to take off. VR is the speed at which you should rotate the aircraft. In PTFS, you will leave the ground at this speed. Finally, V2 is the speed which must be obtained by the end of the runway (at a height of 35 ft) in order to climb during an engine failure. In this calculator, it is assumed to be VR + 4.

What do the landing numbers mean?
For landing, the calculation gives you the actual landing distance that you need, the LDR (which includes a safety margin), and the LDA for the runway you selected. The margin is simply the LDA minus the LDR. VREF is the reference speed, which is the speed you should aim for on touchdown. Your aircraft should never be below this speed during final approach. In this calculator it is 23% above your stall speed. aircraft. VAPP is the approach speed, which should be flown on final approach. In reality, this depends on wind, but in this calculator it is simply VREF + 5.
